Biography

I am a faculty at Wellcome Sanger Institute ,Cambridge Stem Cell Institute ,CCAIM and the University of Cambridge. I am the scientific co-founder of AI VIVO and also a member of ELLIS.

Sanger InstituteCSCICCAIMUniversity of CambridgeAI VIVOELLIS

We aim to leverage AI and advanced experimental techniques to engineer cells and modulate their response to disease and perturbations, and translate them for diagnostics, therapeutics, and drug discovery.

I have received multiple awards for my research (see example) and have been featured in press/journals (see research). Additionally, I have been awarded multiple fellowships, grants, and scholarships, including those from Joachim Hertz, EMBL, Meta, Open Targets and Chan Zuckerberg Initiative. Previously, I completed my PhD in Computational Biology at the Technical University of Munich (TUM) and Helmholtz Munich under the supervision of Fabian Theis.
